Mission & Values

// Why we do what we do

Our Mission

To create open-source tools and resources that preserve cultural knowledge, advance linguistic understanding, promote urban ecology, and foster civic innovation — all while building communities around shared interests and values.

We believe that:

Knowledge disappears faster than we realize
Communities are stronger when they share information
Technology should serve people, not profits
Preservation is an act of resistance
Play and purpose can coexist
Small actions create lasting change

Core Values in Action

01

🌐 Open Everything

We don't just make our work "available" — we make it radically open. Every line of code, every dataset, every process is transparent and forkable. Because knowledge locked away is knowledge that dies.

Example: All OMOD language mappings are CC0 licensed. Take them, improve them, build something amazing. No permission needed.
02

🤝 Community First, Always

We don't helicopter in with solutions. We listen, learn, and build alongside communities. Local knowledge isn't just valuable — it's essential. Every project starts with a conversation, not a blueprint.

Example: Indigenymy works directly with Indigenous language keepers to ensure place names are documented according to their protocols and preferences.
03

🔬 Evidence Over Ideology

We follow the data, not the hype. Every solution is tested, measured, and adjusted based on real-world results. If something doesn't work, we pivot. No ego, just outcomes.

Example: Defrag's urban-wildland recommendations are based on actual wildlife corridor data, not wishful thinking about how animals "should" behave.
04

♻️ Built to Last (Not Just Launch)

We design for decades, not demos. Every project considers long-term maintenance, community ownership, and graceful degradation. Because the flashiest solution is worthless if it breaks next year.

Example: Factso uses simple, portable formats (JSON, markdown) that will be readable 50 years from now, not proprietary databases that vanish with startups.
05

🎯 Action Over Administration

We spend more time building than meeting. Bureaucracy is minimized in favor of making real things that solve real problems. Perfect is the enemy of good enough to help.

Example: SQRRDG prototypes civic ideas quickly with scrappy pilots rather than waiting years for perfect implementation plans.
06

✨ Serious Work, Playful Spirit

Important doesn't mean boring. We believe the best tools are ones people actually want to use. So we add Easter eggs, embrace whimsy, and remember that joy is a feature, not a bug.

Example: Yes, our homepage has floating squirrels and "no blockchain detected" badges. Because if we can't have fun saving the world, what's the point?

Why This Matters Now

In an era of information overload and digital decay, these aren't just nice ideas — they're necessities.

Knowledge Is Vanishing

Every day, websites disappear, languages lose speakers, and local knowledge fades. Link rot claims 38% of web content every decade. We're building lifeboats for information that matters.

Communities Are Fragmenting

Digital tools promised connection but delivered isolation. We create spaces where people collaborate on meaningful work, building real relationships around shared purpose.

Tech Has Lost Its Way

Between surveillance capitalism and blockchain everything, technology forgot its purpose: serving human needs. We're returning to basics — tools that actually help.

Guiding Principles

Start Where You Are

Perfect conditions never come. We begin with what we have, learn as we go, and improve iteratively. Action beats planning paralysis every time.

Embrace the Weird

The best ideas sound ridiculous at first. We welcome unconventional approaches, unexpected connections, and solutions that make people say "wait, that actually works?"

Fail Fast, Learn Faster

Mistakes are data points, not disasters. We prototype quickly, test honestly, and pivot without shame. Every failure teaches us something valuable.

Leave It Better

Every interaction, every project, every line of code should improve something. We're not just avoiding harm — we're actively creating positive change.

Our Vision

We imagine a world where knowledge flows freely, communities thrive through collaboration, and technology serves human flourishing. Where every person can access their cultural heritage, understand any language, live sustainably with nature, and shape their civic environment.

SROC is our small contribution to that future — one preserved document, one mapped sound, one connected habitat, one civic innovation at a time.
